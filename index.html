Your Science-Backed Weekly Gym Routine

This program is designed for balanced muscle growth, strength, and overall fitness. It leverages a high-frequency, scientifically-proven Upper/Lower split to maximize results while managing recovery.

Foundational Philosophy

Upper/Lower Split Rationale

This routine utilizes an Upper/Lower split, hitting each muscle group three times per week. Research by scientists like Dr. Brad Schoenfeld shows that stimulating muscle protein synthesis (MPS) more frequently can lead to superior hypertrophy compared to traditional "body-part splits". Training a muscle group every 48-72 hours keeps it in a more consistent anabolic state, promoting better growth and recovery by not annihilating a single muscle group in one session.

Progressive Overload

This is the cornerstone of all progress. To continuously build muscle and strength, you must consistently challenge your body. Implement this by:
1. Increasing Weight: Once you can comfortably hit the top of your rep range for all sets, add a small amount of weight.
2. Increasing Reps: Aim to add one more rep to your sets each week until you reach the top of the prescribed rep range.
3. Improving Form: Enhancing your technique increases muscle activation and is a form of progression.

Compound vs. Isolation

Our program prioritizes multi-joint compound exercises (squats, presses, rows). These movements are highly efficient, recruiting maximum muscle mass and stimulating a greater hormonal response for growth and strength. Isolation exercises (like curls or extensions) are used strategically to target specific muscles and add volume, but they are supplementary to the core compound lifts.

The Weekly Plan

Warm-up & Cool-down

Warm-up Protocol

Always perform this before your workout to prepare your body and reduce injury risk.

  • General Warm-up (5 min): Light cardio like incline walking, cycling, or elliptical.
  • Dynamic Stretching (5-10 min):
    • Upper Body Days: Band Pull-Aparts, Thoracic Rotations, Arm Circles.
    • Lower Body Days: Leg Swings (forward & sideways), Bodyweight Goblet Squats, Walking Lunges.

Cool-down Protocol

Perform these static stretches post-workout to improve flexibility.

  • Hold each stretch for 30 seconds.
    • Chest Stretch in Doorway
    • Lat Stretch (holding onto a post)
    • Standing Quad Stretch
    • Seated Hamstring Stretch
    • Figure-Four Stretch (for glutes)
